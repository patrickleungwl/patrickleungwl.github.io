    1984

    1: The State

    Winston Smith, 39, is the main protagonist- works in the state's Records department Big Brother is everywhere. BB shows moustache man of about 45. BB sees everything. BB is watching you! Telescreen transmits state news. The Ministry of Truth- Minitrue- occupies a pyramid structure of white concrete, 300 meters high. 

    War is Peace
Freedom is Slavery
Ignorance is Strength

    There are 4 ministries- Minitrue-

    • Truth, Minitrue (propaganda)
    • Peace, Minipax (war)
    • Love, Miniluv (law and order)
    • Plenty, Miniplenty (economics)

    Everyone must wear a face of 'quiet optimism' when facing the Telescreen. Winston set the telescreen at an odd angle so he can write a journal of his thoughts in private.

    There is a daily Two Minutes Hate session where there is a rally to stir up anger and emotion at Emmanuel Goldstein, the Enemy of the People. Everyone goes crazy angry in rally. Everyone gets riled up to kill the enemy of the state. Raw animal emotions come out.

    There is a rumoured underground rebel group called The Brotherhood. Winston suspected O'Brien, a member of the Inner Party, to be a hidden rebel member.

    Momentarily he caught O'Brien's eye.  O'Brien had stood up.  
He had taken off his spectacles and was in the act of resettling 
them on his nose with his characteristic gesture.  But there was 
a fraction of a second when their eyes met, and for as long as it 
took to happen Wiston knew- yes, he KNEW!- that O'Brien was 
thinking the same thing as himself.  An unmistakable message had 
passed.  It was as thought their two minds had opened and the 
thoughts were flowing from one into the other through their eyes.  
'I am with you.'  O'Brien seemed to be saying to him.  'I know 
precisely what you are feeling.  I know all about your contempt, 
your hatred, your disgust.  But don't worry, I am on your side!'  
And then the flash of intelligence was gone, and O'Brien's face 
was as inscrutable as everybody else's.

    Winston writes down Down with Big Brother in his journal.

    2: Kids

    Mrs Parsons, a neighbor and work colleague at the Ministry of Truth- asks for help with fixing the clogged sink. Her kids pester Winston with play charges of 'Thought criminal'. It's only a matter of a 1-2 years before they expose their parent's illegal thoughts to the state police. There is a monthly hanging of war prisoners. Everyone, especially kids, go to watch. Kids are impressionable and trained from the crib to follow the Party. 

    ..they adored the Party and everything connected with it. 
The songs, the processions, the banners, the hiking, the drilling
with dummy rifles, the yelling of slogans, the worship of 
Big Brother- it was all a sort of glorious game to them. 
All their ferocity was turned outwards, against the enemies 
of the State, against foreigners, traitors, saboteurs, 
thought-criminals.  It was almost normal for people over thirty
to be frightened of their own children.  And with good reason,
for hardly a week passed in which 'The Times' did not carry
a paragraph describing how some eavesdropping little sneak- 
'child hero'- had overheard some compromising remark and 
denounced its parents to the Thought Police.

    Winston still wonder about O'Brien. 

    Winston had never been able to feel sure- even after this 
morning's flash of the eyes it was still impossible to be 
sure whether O'Brien was a friend or an enemy.  Nor did it 
seem to matter greatly.  There was a link of understanding
between them, more important than affection or partisanship.
'We shall meet in the place where there is no darkness,' he
had said.  Winston did not know what it meant, only that in
some way or another it would come true.
